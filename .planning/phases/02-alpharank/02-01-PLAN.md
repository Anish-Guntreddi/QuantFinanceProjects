1. Stable per-asset loadings: mom_loading and val_loading drawn once, demeaned and unit-variance normalized.
2. Planted alpha (LOCKED formula): `alpha = IC_target * sigma_noise / sqrt(1 - IC_target**2)` with sigma_noise=monthly_vol. Monthly log-return: `r[t,i] = alpha_mom*mom_loading[i] + alpha_val*val_loading[i] + monthly_vol*rng.standard_normal()`. Because loadings are stable, trailing 12-month price momentum is a noisy proxy for mom_loading — this is what makes the momentum FEATURE (computed from prices in plan 02-02) recover the planted IC. Document this in the module docstring.
3. Delist events: monthly delist prob `1-(1-annual)**(1/12)`; each asset delists at most once; after delist month the asset has NO further rows (truncate its DataFrame — do not emit NaN rows, qbacktest HistoricalDataHandler expects per-symbol frames that simply end).
4. Daily expansion: for each month, split the monthly log-return across its ~21 business days as `daily_log = monthly_log/n_days + eps` where eps is seeded noise (daily vol ≈ monthly_vol/sqrt(21)) recentered so `sum(eps)=0` — daily closes compound EXACTLY to the monthly return. open[t]=close[t-1] (first open = 100.0 base), high=max(open,close)*(1+|e_h|), low=min(open,close)*(1-|e_l|) with small seeded |e| ~ |N(0, 0.002)|, volume = lognormal scaled by a stable per-asset liquidity scale (used by the liquidity factor later).
5. Fundamentals (monthly, per asset): `book_to_market = val_loading[i] + 0.2*rng.standard_normal()` re-drawn each month (slow-moving noisy proxy), `quality = stable quality_loading + noise` with NO planted alpha (quality IC ≈ 0 by construction — honest negative control, document it).
